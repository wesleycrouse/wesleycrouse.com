TIMBR

TIMBR is a Bayesian nonparametric approach for haplotype-based genetic association. It partitions haplotypes into a potentially smaller number of functional alleles, each with independent effect. Haplotypes are partitioned using a Chinese restaurant process, and by leveraging its relationship to the coalescent, it allows for tree-structured haplotype relatedness. This manuscript will be an Editor's Highlight in the Decemeber 2020 issue of Genetics.
